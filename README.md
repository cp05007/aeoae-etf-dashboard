## What AEOAE Is

**AEOAE is a daily ETF market intelligence product built around ETF trend-structure research, signal verification, and reviewable market evidence.**

The product does not try to be a noisy prediction page, a short-term trading call page, or a one-line bullish/bearish signal board. AEOAE is designed as a structured ETF research front end: it organizes market temperature, ETF market breadth, RSI heatmaps, rotation radar, single-ETF structure, historical review, and audit-oriented presentation into one readable experience.

In plain language: **AEOAE helps users read ETF markets with more structure and less guesswork.**

## About Us

AEOAE was built from a simple observation: ETF markets contain a lot of information, but most investors see that information in fragmented form.

One page shows a price chart. Another page shows a ranking table. Another platform shows an indicator. A separate spreadsheet may contain historical review. The user is left to connect everything manually.

AEOAE takes a different approach. It treats ETF research as a complete workflow:

1. Define a structure before the market outcome is known.
2. Present that structure in a readable front-end page.
3. Let the market test it over time.
4. Preserve enough context for later review.
5. Make the result inspectable through ETF detail pages, heatmaps, breadth views, and rotation views.

The purpose is not to make the user blindly believe a signal. The purpose is to make the research chain easier to read, compare, review, and challenge.

## Why It Exists

Modern ETF markets move through layers:

- broad-market pressure
- sector leadership
- theme rotation
- cross-asset risk appetite
- overbought and oversold clusters
- single-ETF structural confirmation
- defensive watch zones
- failed recoveries and weakening structures

AEOAE is designed to give these layers a public, navigable interface.

Instead of asking users to jump between many disconnected tools, AEOAE brings the major observation layers into one ETF research cockpit:

- **Market temperature** tells whether the overall ETF universe is hot, cold, stretched, or quiet.
- **Market breadth** shows how broad the participation is behind a move.
- **RSI heatmap** reveals where pressure and crowding are concentrated.
- **Rotation radar** helps users follow sector rotation, theme rotation, and leadership changes.
- **ETF detail pages** connect broad context back to individual ETFs such as SPY, QQQ, VOO, 510300.SH, and 588000.SH.

## What Makes AEOAE Different

### 1. It focuses on structure, not slogans

Many financial pages reduce market reading to simple labels. AEOAE is built around a more layered reading experience. It presents multiple views so users can observe whether the ETF market is broad or narrow, stretched or compressed, rotating or static, confirming or weakening.

### 2. It emphasizes advance definition and later review

AEOAE's public positioning is based on a clear research principle: **define structure first, let the market validate it later.**

This is the core idea behind the platform's trend-structure research. The public pages are designed to help users see not only what the current view says, but also how a structure can be reviewed after market action unfolds.

### 3. It connects the broad market with single ETF pages

The platform is not only a dashboard. It also provides individual ETF detail pages. This allows a user to move from broad ETF market temperature into a specific ETF's structure, context, and status.

### 4. It is bilingual by design

AEOAE covers both:

- **A-share ETFs** for Chinese ETF market structure research.
- **U.S. ETFs** for English ETF market intelligence and international ETF research.

The bilingual structure makes the product easier to reference from Chinese and English content, research notes, directories, and educational pages.

### 5. It treats auditability as part of product trust

AEOAE is built around the idea that a research system should not only output a conclusion. It should also preserve enough public context for review. This includes front-end presentation, historical review language, ETF detail pages, and public-facing research records.

## Research Philosophy

AEOAE is based on several product principles:

- **Market structure matters more than isolated noise.**
- **Breadth matters because a move with broad participation is different from a narrow move.**
- **Heatmaps matter because crowding and pressure are often easier to see across groups than in one chart.**
- **Rotation matters because leadership changes before many users notice it.**
- **Single ETF pages matter because broad dashboards are not enough for actual ETF research.**
- **Reviewability matters because a research conclusion should face later market behavior.**
- **Front-end clarity matters because users should not need to decode a private spreadsheet to understand the product.**
